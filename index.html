MMAII

MultiMedia Arts II (MMAII) is a course that focuses on a variety of digital media, design principles and technological literacy. We will be learning the Principles of Design, Color Theory and other tools of Graphic Design. We will learn how to use image and video editing software, and a few other tools for creating high quality work. We will also work in the field of Web Design, working with HTML5, CSS3, Javascript and jQuery, in order to create high quality websites. We also will spend time learning how to responsibly use technology, as well as learning new things about the current state of tech.

Senior Capstone

In the ChiTech Senior Capstone students are tasked with creating and starting a business within a small group. Based on student passion and prerequisite coursework, students are placed into one of three courses: Business Startups, Graphic and Web Design, or Computer Programming. Students from each course form interdisciplinary teams ensuring that each has members with the necessary skillsets. Through a variety of creative thinking and problem solving exercises, teams focus on one single, viable business idea. The Design Students, taught by myself, work with the User Experience (UX) approach to design and create product layouts and user interfaces. Business students utilize the Lean Startup methodology of validated learning and iterative experimentation. Programming students work with the Ruby on Rails framework to develop a functioning pre-Alpha level working prototype.

This class is cotaught with Jon Bull (Business) and Kevin Musiorski (Programming).

Projects

Chicago Tech Academy is a Project Based Learnign (PBL) school. Instead of tests or exams, the students must actually create something in the form of a project in order to prove their learning. For instance, my students created an Ad Campaign for a fictional product to learn more about advertising, logo design, rhetoric and video editing. About Me

I am a tech teacher at Chicago Tech Academy. I hold a B.S. in Biological Sciences, and am pursuing a Masters in Software Engineering from DePaul University. I got my start with computers at a young age, and have always been fascinated in the different uses of computers and technology. The impact that a few lines of code can have is outstanding. I went for my Undergrad in Biology and Education, but had helped pay for school by doing some design and web development work on the side. When the opportunity presented itself to teach Tech, I jumped at it, as it helped me realize where my passions really were at.
I am Certified in Biology, Chemistry, and Computer Science. I also have my ESL/ELL Endorsement.
When not at work, I can be found reading, playing games or learning as much as I can about whatever I feel like learning about.
