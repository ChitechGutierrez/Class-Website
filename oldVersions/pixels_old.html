Pixelated Life Project

Mr. Gutierrez's MMAII

2015-2016 Junior/Senior Class

Please note that this page is still in progress. Be Nice.

Project Summary

The students created pixel art animations about something that they found to be personal to them, and helped to shape them into the people they are today. The used the Piskel Web App to make their base drawings and animations.

I started the project out with a short lesson on what pixels are, and the logic behind the terms 8-bit, 16-bit and 32-bit, in terms of computer memory and processing and how it translates into how older video games looked. This included some examples of each, and some student led exploration of different examples. The slideshow below is what was used.

Reflection

The students didn't really dig all that deep to pick what they wanted to animate. Some did, but enough didn't to make me worry about my methods. I realize now that we should have vetted the students' choices earlier in the process. By the time we did, they had already put a day's worth of work in to creating their animation, and most didn't want to have to start over, so they stuck to their original, surface level idea.
