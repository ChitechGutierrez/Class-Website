Butterfly Effect Alternate History Project

Juniors: MMAII/History/English/Art 2015-16

Updated on June 1st, 2016

This project was done in conjunction with English III with Wes Davidson, U.S. History with Tim Morris and Art I with Nicole Dudik. We centered our projects around the ideas put forth by the book 1984 by George Orwell.

English read the book, and pushed to tie everything together. History explored major historical events and the small things that created big changes, and how ideas as spread. Art explored if having limitations can actually cause us to think more creatively. My class, MMAII, explored the flow of information, and how encryption works to protect information. We did a unit on Encryption, followed by starting to write alternate history Choose Your Own Adventure style games. They used research from their History class, and identified a historical event they were interested in. They then identified the key thing that made that event unfold the way it did, and wrote an alternate reality story.

For instance, what if America never got involved in the Middle East following the 9/11 attacks? In that world, America would not have hurt its reputation, and wouldn't have accumulated as much national debt.
